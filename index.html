About the Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique (or Method) is a workflow technique credited to Franesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique is named after traditional kitchen timers that are often designed to look like pomodoro tomatoes.

There are six steps in the original technique:

  • 1) Decide on the task to be done.
  • 2) Set the pomodoro timer (traditionally to 25 minutes).
  • 3) Work on the task.
  • 4) End work when the timer rings; work cycles are counted in the bottom left of your screen.
  • 5) If you have fewer than four checkmarks, take a short break (3–5 minutes), then go to step 2.
  • 6) After four work cycles, take a longer break (15–30 minutes), reset your checkmark count to zero (automatically done for you), then go to step 1.
  • Press play to start every work or break timer.
